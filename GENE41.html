    • Genesis at Home

    Product Design
















    Background

    Genesis, the new luxury brand from Hyundai, offers a complete, end-to-end online sales process to consumers which called 'Genesis at Home'. Consumers can configure their own vehicle, put a deposit, apply for credit, trade their old vehicles and finally get delivered a new vehicle to their front door without visiting a dealership. Based on internal evaluations and user feedback from Genesis, Motoinsight team decided to redesign 'My Vehicles'page and order progress tile view.









    Problem

    For consumers, it was
    - Difficult to know the details of each vehicle
    - Difficult to know order progress on each vehicle
    - Difficult to prioritize each vehicle
    For Genesis team, it was
    - Difficult to know which vehicle is currently in the process of buying or which are saved for pure research








    Objective & Approach

    The main objective with this new UI and functional treatment is to enhance the user experience by bringing more focus on what vehicle the consumer is in the process of buying, where in the order progress they are currently, and give the consumer a central "hub" experience for all their Genesis vehicles/orders.



    To do so, the team decide to categorize orders into two types; 'Current Purchases'(later: 'Active Orders') which is a list of vehicles that are currently in the process of being ordered and 'Saved Vehicles' which is a list of vehicles that have completed build and purchase step.








    User Testing

    With a first version of prototype, the team conduct a user testing with 2 power users and 4 medium-savvy users. Here are some questions from the user test.
    - "What do you think it's the differences between 'saved vehicle' vs. 'current purchase'? - "Can you 'activate' an order?" - “Can you tell me which accessories you have added to your current purchase?” - “How far in the order process are you on the current purchase?” - “How would you change the colour of the active purchase?” - “Based on the current status on your purchase, how would you proceed with the next step?” - “How would you add a new vehicle?” - “How would you add a protection plan to the first vehicle on your saved vehicle list?” - “From the list of saved vehicles, remove the second vehicle from the bottom”








    Findings & Iteration

    After the user testing, the team found out that the visual treatment between 'Current Purchases' and 'Saved Vehicle' should be more distinct. Also, the label 'Current Purchases' was confusing for the most participants. For the order progress, most of participants can figure out which step their order is in, but wanted to know more information about what to do next.
    So, the team decide to update the 'version 1' to 'version 2' as the following image

    First of all, 'Current Purchases' become 'Active Orders' so it's more specifict to what's active or not for consumers. Combining the 'tile view' and 'My Vehicles' page into one central dashboard differenciate the visual treatment between the 'Active Orders' and 'Saved Vehicles'. It also enbles to see the whole order process at a glance.








    Mobile UI

    The new solution is fully responsive for a seamless user experience. Consumers can start their research/car buying journey from wherever they want and whenever they want from their hand-held devices and desktop.








    Micro Animation

    As a part of this product imporvement, micro animations are added for more delightful user experience.
