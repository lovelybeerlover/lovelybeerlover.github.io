    Product Concept Design X Design Thesis | Jan - April 2014

    This is a design thesis project from Interaction Design and Development Program at George Brown College. 'Wee' is a product + service that enable the young generation to communicate with their grandparents easier. The product comes with a digital frame that has wifi and local storage , and an mobile app that can send media contents from users mobile phone to the digital frame.
















    Concept

    ‘Wee’ consists of two components: a digital frame and a mobile app. The yonger generation in the family who are familiar to use smartphones and smarts technologies sends media contents such as photos and videos by using the WEE mobile app on their own phones. Then their grandparents gets those media contents on their 'Wee Frame' and they can browse them on it. WEE is a bridge to connect the young generation and the old generation by using simple technologies.











    Background & Challenges

    One summer, I was hanging out with my cousin in Toronto, who is studing in NYC. We took a selfie together and sent it to our parents and other cousins who has a messenging app and also posted it on our social media. The same year in winter, I was visiting my grandmother in South Korea, then realized she didn't have any idea what happene that summer. We bought her an ipad and gave access to my facebook but still she was isolated. Table PCs have too many functions, so as social media like facebook for someone like my grandma. That was the moment I realized we need something simple and inclusive solution for senior citizens who want to stay connected with yonger family members.

    UX Flow on Mobile App

    Sender and Receiver are two types of users by the way they use the system. Sender is a user who creates and sends the contents from their mobile devices to WEE frame. Receiver is a user who has an WEE frame and receives all the contents from Senders.
